Mtendele Baptist Church Doctrine

The Universal Love of God

We believe that God's love is unconditional and extends to all people (John 3:16). Mtendele Baptist Church preaches love as the foundation of faith and encourages believers to express love towards all humanity.

The Universality of Salvation in Jesus Christ

Salvation is available to all who call upon the name of Jesus (Romans 10:13). We teach that Christ's sacrifice is for all, and no one is beyond redemption.

The Unity of All Believers

The body of Christ is one, and all believers are called to live in unity (John 17:21). Mtendele Baptist Church works to break denominational barriers and promote spiritual harmony.

The Authority of the Holy Scriptures

The Bible is the inspired Word of God and the ultimate authority on all matters of faith and practice (2 Timothy 3:16). The Church follows biblical doctrine above human traditions.

The Power of the Holy Spirit

We believe in the baptism of the Holy Spirit, the operation of spiritual gifts, and the empowerment of believers for ministry (Acts 1:8).

Global Evangelism and Discipleship

The Church is committed to fulfilling the Great Commission by spreading the Gospel to all nations (Matthew 28:19-20). Evangelism is a core duty of every believer.

Sacraments and Ordinances

The sacred acts instituted by our Lord Jesus Christ as outward signs of inward grace and powerful expressions of our faith.

Baptism

Baptism is the sacrament of initiation into the Christian faith. It symbolizes the washing away of sin, a new birth in Christ, and entry into the Body of Christ. It is done with water and in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.

"Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit." (Matthew 28:19)

Holy Communion

Holy Communion is the remembrance of Christ's sacrifice. Through bread and wine (or alternatives), believers share in the body and blood of Christ spiritually. It is a continual reminder of His love and the new covenant.

"Do this in remembrance of me." (Luke 22:19)

Marriage

Marriage is the holy union between a man and a woman, established by God. It reflects the relationship between Christ and the Church and is blessed for love, companionship, and procreation.

"Therefore a man shall leave his father and his mother and hold fast to his wife, and they shall become one flesh." (Genesis 2:24)

1 Lordship of Jesus Christ

We believe and confess that Jesus Christ is the Son of the Living God, the Savior of the world, the Head of the Church, and the only way to eternal life. This pillar upholds the universal call of salvation for all humanity through faith in Jesus Christ.

Every activity, teaching, and mission of the Church is rooted in the Lordship, example, and teachings of Christ. Through this pillar, the Church will continually proclaim the Gospel of Grace and extend the invitation of salvation without discrimination.

"And Jesus came and said to them, 'All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me.'" (Matthew 28:18)

"If you confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved." (Romans 10:9)

2 Holy Scriptures

We believe that the Holy Bible, both Old and New Testaments, is the inspired, infallible, and authoritative Word of God. The Church is committed to teaching, preaching, and practicing the Word of God as the supreme rule of faith, doctrine, and life.

This pillar promotes continuous Bible Study, Discipleship Programs, and sound theological teaching that nurtures believers toward spiritual maturity. It calls every believer to live according to biblical truth, with love, holiness, and integrity.

"All Scripture is breathed out by God and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness." (2 Timothy 3:16)

3 Global Mission

This pillar emphasizes the church's responsibility to spread the gospel worldwide and supporting missions, engaging in cross cultural evangelism and participating in the advancement of God's kingdom on earth. It includes outreach to people of all nations and cultures with an understanding of universal salvation and the church's role in fulfilling the greater commission.

"Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit." (Matthew 28:19)

"But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you, and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the end of the earth." (Acts 1:8)

4 Spiritual Empowerment and Ministry

Mtendele Baptist Church is committed to a Spirit-led ministry where believers are empowered to serve God and humanity. We believe in the baptism of the Holy Spirit, the operation of spiritual gifts, signs, wonders, and the five-fold ministry (Apostles, Prophets, Evangelists, Pastors, and Teachers).

This pillar promotes discipleship, leadership development, evangelism, and missions. It also encourages every believer to discover, develop, and use their spiritual gifts for the edification of the Church and the expansion of God's Kingdom.

"But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you, and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the end of the earth." (Acts 1:8)

"For God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control." (2 Timothy 1:7)

5 Compassion and Social Responsibility

The Church is called not only to preach but also to demonstrate the love of Christ through acts of mercy, compassion, and justice. This pillar drives the Church's mission to care for the poor, orphans, widows, marginalized, and vulnerable groups.

It includes initiatives like education support, healthcare, economic empowerment, peace advocacy, and community development and all people within and outside the church where the church see can assist. Through this pillar, the Church becomes the salt and light of the world, addressing both spiritual and physical needs.

"For I was hungry and you gave me food, I was thirsty and you gave me drink, I was a stranger and you welcomed me." (Matthew 25:35)

"Religion that is pure and undefiled before God the Father is this: to visit orphans and widows in their affliction, and to keep oneself unstained from the world." (James 1:27)

6 Prayer, Worship, and Holiness

We believe that prayer, worship, and holiness are the lifeblood of the Church. This pillar calls every believer to live a consecrated life, constantly seeking God's face through personal and corporate prayer, fasting, praise, and worship.

It encourages a culture of intimacy with God, repentance, and walking in righteousness. The Church will establish prayer altars, organize worship gatherings, and raise a praying, fasting, and holy people who will stand in the gap for nations, families, and individuals.

"God is spirit, and those who worship him must worship in spirit and truth." (John 4:24)

"But as he who called you is holy, you also be holy in all your conduct, since it is written, 'You shall be holy, for I am holy.'" (1 Peter 1:15-16)

7 The Pillar of Universal Love

"For God so loved the world..." (John 3:16). Mtendele Baptist Church stands on the foundation of God's unconditional love for all humanity. We believe that every person, regardless of race, tribe, nation, social status, or past sins, is deeply loved by God and is called into fellowship with Him.

We promote the love of God as a universal gift, not limited to denominations or religious labels. This pillar calls us to reflect God's love by practicing compassion, forgiveness, hospitality, mercy, and peace with all people. Our mission is to break barriers and unite people under God's love.

"For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life." (John 3:16)

"There is neither Jew nor Gentile, neither slave nor free, nor is there male and female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus." (Galatians 3:28)

8 The Pillar of True Worship

"God is Spirit, and those who worship Him must worship in spirit and truth." (John 4:24). Worship is the heartbeat of Mtendele Baptist Church. This pillar emphasizes the importance of offering genuine, spirit-led, and truth-based worship to God.

Worship is not confined to forms, buildings, or rituals, but flows from a heart fully surrendered to God. We encourage worship that touches both heaven and earth, blending prayer, praise, thanksgiving, music, fasting, and sacrificial living. We believe worship should transform lives, draw people closer to God, and manifest God's presence among us.

"Yet a time is coming and has now come when the true worshipers will worship the Father in the Spirit and in truth, for they are the kind of worshipers the Father seeks." (John 4:23)

"Therefore, I urge you, brothers and sisters, in view of God's mercy, to offer your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and pleasing to Godâ€”this is your true and proper worship." (Romans 12:1)

9 The Pillar of Apostolic Doctrine

"They continued steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine and fellowship..." (Acts 2:42). We are grounded in the pure doctrine of Christ as taught by the apostles and revealed in the Holy Scriptures.

This pillar commits us to uphold sound biblical teaching, to guard against false doctrines, and to teach the full counsel of God with integrity and clarity. We teach the Word of God to equip believers for spiritual growth, maturity, and fruitful living. The Apostolic Doctrine covers the foundational truths: Salvation by grace through faith, repentance, baptism, the Holy Spirit, holiness, the resurrection, and eternal life.

"They devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching and to fellowship, to the breaking of bread and to prayer." (Acts 2:42)

"Hold fast the pattern of sound words which you have heard from me, in faith and love which are in Christ Jesus." (2 Timothy 1:13)

10 The Pillar of Evangelism and Mission

"Go therefore and make disciples of all nations..." (Matthew 28:19-20). Evangelism is the lifeline of the Church. We are called to reach the unreached, revive the broken, and restore the lost.

This pillar drives us to proclaim the gospel of Jesus Christ to every person, tribe, and nation. Mtendele Baptist Church is a mission-driven movement, aiming to carry the gospel to villages, towns, cities, and the nations without discrimination. Through preaching, personal witness, social outreach, and acts of mercy, we seek to make disciples and expand the Kingdom of God universally.

"Then Jesus came to them and said, 'All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. Therefore go and make disciples of all nations...'" (Matthew 28:18-19)

"How, then, can they call on the one they have not believed in? And how can they believe in the one of whom they have not heard? And how can they hear without someone preaching to them?" (Romans 10:14)

11 The Pillar of Holiness and Righteous Living

"Be holy, for I am holy." (1 Peter 1:16). Mtendele Baptist Church is committed to a lifestyle of holiness and righteousness. We believe that the grace of God empowers believers not only to be saved but also to live victorious and holy lives.

This pillar calls all believers to live separate from sin, pursue purity, integrity, justice, and be examples of Christ in the world. Holiness is not legalism, but a joyful response to God's love, demonstrated in our character, conduct, and relationships.

"But just as he who called you is holy, so be holy in all you do; for it is written: 'Be holy, because I am holy.'" (1 Peter 1:15-16)

"Make every effort to live in peace with everyone and to be holy; without holiness no one will see the Lord." (Hebrews 12:14)

12 The Pillar of Unity and Fellowship

"That they all may be one, as You, Father, are in Me, and I in You..." (John 17:21). Unity is a major characteristic of the Church. Mtendele Baptist Church is called to promote the unity of believers, not only within itself but across the Body of Christ globally.

We believe in fellowship, love, and working together for the glory of God. This pillar teaches us to overcome divisions caused by tribalism, class, ethnicity, or personal ambitions. We aim to model the unity of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit in how we relate with one another, with other churches, and with the wider community.

"Behold, how good and pleasant it is when brothers dwell in unity!" (Psalm 133:1)

"Make every effort to keep the unity of the Spirit through the bond of peace." (Ephesians 4:3)
